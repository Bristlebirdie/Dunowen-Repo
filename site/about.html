About Us

There comes a point in time when you have to stop enviously watching re-runs of Escape to the Country and River Cottage, a time when you realize it’s now or never. That realization came at the end of August 2013, when on a chance visit to Clonakilty we came across Dunowen House. On a stunningly beautiful Summer’s day, we came over the hill from Ardfield, spotted Galley Head lighthouse on the horizon and instantly fell in love with the area. By the time we’d reached the top of sweeping driveway and arrived at the front door, we knew we’d found “the place”. Within two weeks we’d sold our house in Dublin and within six weeks were loading three children, a dog, two trucks and heading for our own “escape to the country” adventure. Looking back it definitely was a whirlwind.

Saying goodbye to office based careers in marketing and finance in Dublin city has not been difficult. I think our keen interest in cooking, baking, gardening and our kids hobbies of sailing and horse-riding have made the transition even easier.

Opening Dunowen House as a self-catering and boutique guesthouse business gives us the opportunity to share this wonderful slice of Irish life with our guests. Over the next 18 months, we will undertake a renovation project to convert the old coach house, barn and garden cottage, adding further self-catering accommodation at Dunowen. We are currently re-instating the traditional walled gardens and orchard, to provide us with ample fresh organic produce on site. Check our blog for regular progress updates.

When we first told friends and family we were selling up in Dublin and moving to West Cork, the most frequent response was “You’re very brave”. I think often what they really wanted to say was “Are you crazy?” We now know we would have been crazy not to take that leap of faith and move to Dunowen House! We think once you visit, you’ll agree.

Originally built in the late 1700’s by Captain George Sandes, Dunowen has had many colourful inhabitants over the years. Each generation of owner has left its signature on the house and gardens, to be enjoyed by our guests today. Sands Cove at the end of our laneway, took its name from the original Sandes family, dropping the “e” over time, while the walled garden features the original bell tower that called the seven gardeners to meals.

Mention Dunowen House now to anyone living in the environs of Clonakilty and they immediately associate it with a more recent resident. For more than 30 years Noel Redding, bass player with the Jimi Hendrix Experience, called Dunowen House home, where he entertained music legends throughout the 70’s, 80’s and 90’s. More recently, Dunowen was owned by 1980’s popstar Nik Kershaw.

We have dedicated a large room in the house to various rock and roll memorabilia linked to Noel Redding, specifically original concert posters, vintage magazines and music. All to be enjoyed by our guests while they stay.
